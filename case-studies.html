CNC machining case studies

Examples of the machining problems we help buyers solve.

Many CNC projects fail not because the supplier cannot cut metal, but because tolerance, deformation, process sequence, inspection and communication were not clarified early enough. These case-style examples show how Jintat thinks through precision machining projects.

Thin-Wall Stainless Sleeve

For thin-wall stainless parts, deformation control is often more important than cutting speed.

  • Material: SUS304 / stainless steel
  • Challenge: thin wall, roundness and parallelism control
  • Approach: fixture planning, rough/finish sequence, inspection focus
  • Proof: CMM / dimensional verification available
Automation Fixture Component

Automation and machine builders need repeatable parts that assemble smoothly, not just parts that look finished.

  • Material: Aluminum / steel depending on drawing
  • Application: fixture, bracket, tooling, machine component
  • Approach: DFM review before production
  • Support: prototype to low-volume production
Precision Aluminum Component

Aluminum parts often require fast turnaround, clean surfaces and stable dimensional control.

  • Material: 6061-T6 / 7075 depending on project
  • Process: milling, drilling, tapping, finishing coordination
  • Use: equipment parts, structures, robotics components
  • Support: surface treatment coordination available
CMM Inspection Focus

For overseas buyers, inspection evidence reduces risk before parts enter assembly.

  • Equipment: Mitutoyo CMM, Keyence measurement support
  • Focus: critical dimensions and functional features
  • Deliverable: inspection report support by request
  • Goal: fewer surprises during assembly
Prototype to Low-Volume

Early-stage engineering projects need flexibility, feedback and quick iteration.

  • Stage: sample, prototype, pilot build, low volume
  • Support: tolerance clarification and manufacturability review
  • Strength: high-mix project handling
  • Goal: reduce supplier coordination risk
One-Stop Coordination

A good supplier should help manage more than machining alone when the project requires it.

  • Support: CNC turning and milling
  • Coordination: finishing, inspection, simple assembly as needed
  • Communication: clear RFQ and drawing review process
  • Buyer benefit: fewer vendors to manage
